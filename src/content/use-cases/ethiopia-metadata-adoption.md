Standards only matter if someone outside the founding team uses them. In early
2026 the Ethiopian Meteorology Institute (EMI) became the first national
service to align its climate data catalogue to the
[CDH metadata standard](/wikis/cdh-metadata-standard).

## Why EMI did it

EMI maintains hundreds of observational and derived layers, described in
formats that had drifted apart over a decade. Adopting an external, versioned
standard was cheaper than writing and governing their own — and it made their
holdings automatically legible to regional partners already using the Hub.

## The work

A two-week mapping sprint aligned EMI's internal fields to the CDH schema,
with the [STAC profile](/wikis/stac-profile) generated from the records. Most
fields mapped one-to-one; the main effort went into writing the
appropriate-use guidance the standard requires, which EMI's scientists ended
up valuing most.

## Where it stands

Over forty layers are now published with validated records and STAC items.
Regional guidance documents have since referenced the profile as an
implementation example — a small sign the standard is travelling beyond
CGIAR.
